Drawing

  • Concerns
    • How the head and jaw overlaps with the neck to connect to the torso.
    • How the breasts attach to and hang from the torso.
    • Proportions and perspective of the head and jaw and breasts.
    • I think it’s interesting to think of figure drawing as a composition of boxes. Consider the proportions and perspectives of the boxes. How the boxes overlap and connect. The lighting of the boxes. Intentional curves and distortions.
    • Prerequisite is figure drawing.
      • Anatomy studies.
      • How clothes wrap and drape from the body.
  • Stages
    • Composition exploration (e.g., thumbnailing).
    • Rough.
    • Shape refinement.
    • Value and color.
    • Rendering (e.g., details, line, edge, etc.).
  • For each stage
    • Time limited.
  • Before each stage except exploration
    • Drawing study.
  • After the last stage
    • Judge each stage in relation to the whole.
    • Identify the weakest stage.
      • Bonus improvement stage.
  • Pacing
    • Try one stage per day.
  • Comments
    • The difficulty of each stage depends on the project. Composition has the greatest variance in difficulty.
    • I should focus on building from reference. It’s difficult to apply many transformations simultaneously so start from one, then a few, and build build build. Eventually, acquiring and combining transformations will be a simple thing.
    • The goal is to create a drawing where there are aspects that are more appealing to me than the corresponding aspects in the reference.
